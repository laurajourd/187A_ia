Welcome to our Information Architecture Project

The objective of this assignment is to understand the constraints and freedom involved in choosing an information architecture.

What is information architecture?

"Information architecture is the structure or organization of information in an information space. On the Web, information architecture is a combination of organizing a site's content into categories and creating an interface to support those categories. The step from information architecture to navigation is not trivial: the two address slightly different concerns, since navigation is centrally concerned with the visual display of information, whereas information architecture is centrally concerned with the logical or conceptual organization of information. An effective information architecture is key to more usable websites."

The two main goals of this project are: on the one hand, to understand the different features of a good information architecture. On the other hand, to engage in the process of designing an effective organizational structure. We seek to achieve these goals by redesigning the top and secondary levels of the information architecture for the University of Louisiana, Lafayette website.

In these pages we present four deliverables:

  1. User Personas and Scenarios: We create five different user types, as well as three scenarios for each user.
  2. Competitive Analysis: A comparison of navigational structures of five different university websites, presented in table format.
  3. Redesign: We focus on the main navigation and the secondary level pages.
  4. Hardest Decisions: An analysis of our hardest design decisions.
