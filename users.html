Shelly McDaniel

[Back to top]


Shelly just started her senior year of high school and will be applying for colleges this Fall. She is considering the University of Louisiana, Lafayette because it is in-state, which is important in terms of both distance and cost. She has watched her three older siblings leave for college, and they all stayed close to home. She is very close with her family, and although she won't admit it to her friends, she's looking forward to bringing home her laundry and having her mom pack her copious amounts of food to bring back with her.

Shelly's grandfather taught her how to play chess when she was four years old, and she is now a nationally ranked chess player. She is extremely interested in the legal system and hopes to become a lawyer, although she is unsure exactly what type of law she wants to specialize in. She is equally ambitious in her social goals, hoping not only to meet her future bridesmaids but also begin the search for the elusive Mr. Right.

Scenario 1

Scenario Context

Shelly is comparing the pre-law programs at each of the different colleges she is interested in applying to.

Scenario Question

What kind of pre-law programs exist at the University of Louisiana?

User Task

Find out what pre-law program is offered at the University of Louisiana.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Student Life" on the primary navigation
  3. Click on "Academic Programs" under "Academic Programs"
  4. Select "L" from the list of letters under "Departments by Alphabet"
  5. Click on "(See Political Science)" link next to "Law"
  6. Explore the Political Science Dept's website

Scenario 2

Scenario Context

Shelly is at her grandparents' house for Sunday dinner, and her grandfather mentions that he played in many intercollegiate chess tournaments as a college student. Shelly wonders if the University of Louisiana, Lafayette has a chess team.

Scenario Question

Is there a chess club or organization I can join?

User Task

Find a list of student organizations at UL Lafayette and search for something related to chess.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Student Life" on the primary navigation
  3. Click on "Student Organization Information" under "Campus Organizations"
  4. Click on "Join an Organization" on the main navigation of the Student Organizations website
  5. Click on "Organization List –alpha order"/li>
  6. Use Command +F to search the page and type in "Chess"
  7. No result found
  8. Type in "louisiana.edu" in the browser to get back to the main site
  9. Click on Student Life on the primary navigation
  10. Click Search
  11. Type in "chess club"
  12. 7 results found no chess club

Scenario 3

Scenario Context

Shelly has decided to apply to UL Lafayette, and she knows that many schools offer scholarships, in addition to all the private scholarships out there. She definitely plans to apply for scholarships and file her FAFSA, and she knows the FAFSA isn't available until after January 1st. She isn't sure what scholarships UL Lafayette offers, or when the deadlines are this year.

Scenario Question

What scholarships can Shelly apply for and how does she apply?

User Task

Find a list of scholarships and application instructions and deadlines.

User Steps

  1. Go to Home Page
  2. Click on link for Prospective Students
  3. Click on "Freshman Students"
  4. Click on "Scholarships" under "Money Matters"
  5. Click on "High School Seniors"

Charles Lacarin

[Back to top]


Charles comes from France and is admitted to spend a year at UL Lafayette. He has two major concerns: finding some intensive English courses in order to improve his level and finding out more about university housing, which he has been told is quite cheap. He is in his third year of mechanical engineering and would like to find an internship through the university. Charles enjoys gymnastics and swimming in his free time.

Scenario 1

Scenario Context

Charles would like to live in university housing in order to meet new people and experience the UL Lafayette student life.

Scenario Question

Who is eligible to live on campus? Are the dining facilities open every day for students who live on campus? How much is room and board?

User Task

Find a list of university housing and dining options, as well as instructions on how to apply to live on campus, if necessary.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Student Life" on the primary navigation
  3. Go to "Other Services" at the bottom of the page and then click on "Housing"
  4. Click on "Room and Board Rate" on the top navigation bar
  5. Download the .pdf files to read about rates and meal plans
  6. Click on "Resource Center" on the top navigation
  7. Click on "Applications and Forms"
  8. Download the appropriate application

Scenario 2

Scenario Context

Charles likes practicing gymnastics. He would like to continue taking gymnastics twice a week, as he does in France. He wants to know if there is a gymnastics program at UL Lafayette.

Scenario Question

What recreational sports programs are available at UL Lafayette? If there are gymnastics classes, how much do they cost and when are they offered?

User Task

Find a list of recreational sports classes offered, along with cost and schedule.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Athletics" on the main navigation
  3. Then click on "Club Sports." Gymnastics is not on the list.
  4. Click on Aerobics on the left navigation bar.
  5. Feel a bit disappointed. Unfortunately, UL Lafayette does not offer gymnastics, although they do offer free fitness classes.

Scenario 3

Scenario Context

Although disappointed that the university does not have a gymnastics program, Charles remembers that one of his major goals is to improve his English. He would like to find an intensive English program in order to make quick progress.

Scenario Question

Does UL Lafayette offer English courses for second-language learners? If so, how can Charles sign up for an intensive English course?

User Task

Search for intensive English courses for second-language learners, and sign up for a class of the appropriate level, if available.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Search" on the home page and enter "English program"
  3. Select the first link "Intensive English Program"
  4. Click on "Application and Admission" to see who is eligible.
  5. Go to the online application form and begin filling out the application.

Nicolas Fedorczak

[Back to top]


Nicolas is a French guy and currently looking for a post-doctoral research position in the physics field. He has 3 different universities in mind, including UL Lafayette. In order to make the right choice, Nicolas wants to know more about each university: mainly, the social life and the academic research opportunities in the physics field. He likes playing guitar and going to the beach with his friends. Nicolas would like to meet new friends and discover the American culture.

Scenario 1

Scenario Context

Nicolas is just beginning to look into the three different universities he has in mind. First, he wants to gather some basic information about each university.

Scenario Question

What is the background of UL Lafayette? How can he contact the university?

User Task

Get an overview of basic information about UL Lafayette. Find contact information.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "About us" on the primary navigation
  3. Click on "Fast Facts about UL Lafayette": get an overview of UL Lafayette
  4. Click on "Contact Us" above the main navigation bar
  5. Click on "Telephone Directory" to get the main campus phone number

Scenario 2

Scenario Context

As a post doctoral research in physics, Nicolas would like to find more information about the physics research at UL Lafayette and choose the perfect and interesting research project.

Scenario Question

What are the current subjects in physics? Is there a complete description and e-mail in order to contact the person in charge? Is there indication about the salary?

User Task

Find a description of all post doctoral research opportunities in the physics department, including contact information of the primary investigator

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Research" on the primary navigation
  3. Click on "Centers and Laboratories": there does not seem to be a physics lab on the list
  4. Go back to the University Website
  5. Click on "Academics" on the primary navigation
  6. Click on "Colleges & Departments / Departments A - Z / Majors A - Z"
  7. Click on "Sciences"
  8. Click on "Physics"
  9. Click on "Research" on the primary navigation of the Physics Department homepage: the page is under construction
  10. Click on "Contact Us" at the top right corner of the page: Contact information for the Physics Department

Scenario 3

Scenario Context

Nicolas likes playing music and meeting new people. Therefore, he is interested in learning more about the area surrounding the university.

Scenario Question

Where can I go out to listen to music, see some concerts and meet people.

User Task

Find information about the area surrounding the university.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Prospective Students"’ on the home page
  3. Select "Area Profile" on the top red navigation bar
  4. Click on "Culture": list of direct links for different activities in the city of Lafayette, organized by type

Mary McDaniel

[Back to top]


Mary is a stay-at-home mom, although recently she began taking online classes to brush up on her accounting skills. She has served as the President of the Booster Club at her children's high school for the last three years, and she was Treasurer of the Parents' Association before that.

Having already sent three of her four children off to college, Mary knows this process can be confusing and stressful. She believes that choosing the right college is a critical first step toward adulthood, but, as any parent knows, it's always a little hard to let them go.

Her top concerns are finances, campus health and safety, and career services.

Scenario 1

Scenario Context

<

Mary is planning a college trip for her daughter, and she knows from her previous experience that some colleges require advance reservation for campus tours. Her youngest is currently in her junior year, so the trip is scheduled for Spring break.

Scenario Question

Are campus tours offered in April? If so, how can I make a reservation?

User Task

Find a schedule of campus tours and make a reservation for the desired date, if necessary.

User Steps

  1. Go to the university site
  2. Click on link to Visitors portal:
  3. Click on "Campus Tours"
  4. Click on "Schedule Your Tour Online!"
  5. Choose date and time desired
  6. Create an account with eCampusTours.com
  7. Login to eCampusTours.com --> Advanced college search page
  8. Return to UL Lafayette tours
  9. Fill out and submit registration form

Scenario 2

Scenario Context

After visiting a few colleges, her daughter has decided on 7 different schools to apply to. Mary is doing a preliminary review of the tuition and fees of each school.

Scenario Question

What is the total cost for an undergraduate student, including housing and dining, for the current schoolyear, and what is the projected amount for next year?

User Task

Find a breakdown of undergradaute tuition and fees for the current and, if possible, future schoolyears.

User Steps

  1. Go to university site
  2. Click on link to "Prospective Students" portal
  3. Click on "Freshman Students"
  4. Click on "Estimated Costs"

Scenario 3

Scenario Context

Mary's daughter has received her acceptance letters and now needs to make her final decision. Mary knows her daughter is mainly concerned about campus life, but Mary wants her daughter to attend a school that also provides ample opportunity to explore different career paths.

Scenario Question

What kind of career services does the school offer, especially in regards to helping students find internship opportunities?

User Task

Locate the website for the career services center, if there is one, and find information on student internships.

User Steps

  1. Go to university site
  2. Click on the "Current Students" portal
  3. Click on "Career Services" under "Other Services"
  4. Click on "Families"
  5. Click on "Overview of Services"

Dr. Abdul Mohammad

[Back to top]


Dr. Abdul Mohammad has spent the last 20 years working at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. Now at the young age of 90, he is ready to inqure about retirement. Over the years, he has lived all over the world, from Saudi Arabia to Macau, but considers Louisiana my home. His children currently have their own families in New York City, and he plans to visit them after this semester ends. Some of his hobbies include teaching, reading, golf, and taking long walks with his dogs. He is also a patron of the arts, particularly sculpture and painting. He often visits the gallery on campus and was thinking of volunteering after retirement.

Scenario 1

Scenario Context

Dr. Mohammad is planning to retire soon and wants to find out more on organizing his exit from the University of Louisiana at Lafayette.

Scenario Question

How does Dr. Mohammad get started with the retirement process?

User Task

Find information and resources regarding retirement for faculty members.

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Faculty and Staff"
  3. Under "Services and Facilities," click on "Human Resources"
  4. Identify the staff members who deal with retirement

Scenario 2

Scenario Context

Dr. Mohammad is wondering if the Chemistry department has any additional information for retiring professors. He wants to be sure he is taken care of when he retires.

Scenario Question

What faculty resources are available on the Chemistry webpage?

User Task

Navigate to the chemistry page from the main home page find some employee resources.

User Steps

  1. Go to the home page
  2. Click on "Academics" on primary navigation
  3. Click on "Undergraduate and Graduate Programs: Colleges and Departments"
  4. Click on "C" under "Departments by Alphabet"
  5. Click on "Chemistry"
  6. Click on "Faculty & Staff": a list of faculty and staff, rather than resources for faculty

Scenario 3

Scenario Context

Being the lover of arts that he is, Dr. Mohammad is wondering about possibly opportunities volunteering at the arts department at the university.

Scenario Question

Are any of the university art galleries accepting volunteers? Any art museums on campus accepting volunteers?

User Task

Find information about university art galleries or museums and learn about volunteer opportunities

User Steps

  1. Go to the Home Page
  2. Click on "Student Life"
  3. Click on "Art Museum"
  4. Roll over the blue circles
  5. Click on the blue circle for "Support UAM": membership information
  6. Go back to the UAM homepage
  7. Click on the blue circle for "Contact"
  8. Call or write the museum to find out about volunteer opportunities
