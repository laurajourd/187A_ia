Welcome in our Information Architecture's Project.

The objective of this assignment is to understand the constraints and freedom involved in choosing an information architecture.

What does information architecture mean?

"Information architecture is the structure or organization of information in an information space. On the Web, information architecture is a combination of organizing a site's content into categories and creating an interface to support those categories. The step from information architecture to navigation is not trivial: the two address slightly different concerns, since navigation is centrally concerned with the visual display of information, whereas information architecture is centrally concerned with the logical or conceptual organization of information. An effective information architecture is key to more usable Wesites".

The two main goals are: on the one hand, to understand the different features involved in creating a good Information Architecture. On the other hand, to look at examples of good organizational structure.
